## 🎯 About the Workshop

Radiotherapy (RT) is a cornerstone of modern oncology, accounting for approximately 40% of curative cancer treatments. RT uses targeted radiation to destroy cancer cells; however, healthy tissues in the radiation path may also be affected. The fundamental challenge is therefore to precisely deliver dose to the tumor while protecting nearby organs. Therefore, unlike diagnostic imaging, where the primary goal is detection, RT operates as a complex, multi-stage therapeutic ecosystem. It requires the seamless integration of longitudinal imaging, precise anatomical definition, radiation physics, and biological response modelling.

The "black-box" nature of deep learning is particularly high-stakes in RT, where algorithmic decisions directly dictate the physical delivery of high-dose radiation. A geometric error in AI-based contouring or a hallucination in image synthesis does not merely result in a misdiagnosis; it can lead to catastrophic geographic misses, critically reducing the chance of curing the disease and/or increasing the risk of severe side effects. 

The **MIART Workshop** aims to establish a centralized home for the diverse and growing community of researchers applying AI to radiation oncology. We argue that the next generation of therapeutic AI must be "physics-aware" and "biologically-driven," bridging the gap between data-driven discovery and clinical intervention to foster a holistic approach to Personalized Radiotherapy (PRT).

## 📝 Call for Papers

We invite submissions covering a broad range of topics across the therapeutic workflow, including but not limited to:

- **Therapeutic Image Synthesis & Reconstruction:** Methods for synthetic CT generation, cross-modality translation, and artifact reduction specifically designed for treatment planning and guidance.
- **Target Definition & Geometric Modeling:** Robust segmentation of tumors and organs-at-risk, including uncertainty-aware contouring and handling of anatomical variability.
- **Treatment Optimization & Automation:** AI-driven approaches for inverse planning, dose/delivery optimization, and automated quality assurance, including those approaches that rely on previously delivered dose (e.g., adaptive RT or reirradiation).
- **Biophysical Modeling & Response Prediction:** Integrating biological priors into deep learning to predict toxicity, tumor control, and patient-specific treatment outcomes.
- **Adaptive Therapy & Motion Management:** Techniques for longitudinal monitoring, real-time tracking, and online adaptation to anatomical changes during the treatment course.
- **Data Scarcity & In Silico Modeling:** Strategies for handling limited datasets, including digital twins, biomechanical simulation, and synthetic data generation.
- **Clinical Translation & Safety:** Frameworks for interpretability, error propagation analysis, and ensuring the safety of AI deployment in high-stakes clinical environments.

### Submission & Review Process
- **Format:** Manuscripts should be 8-10 pages (including references), formatted and anonymized according to the [Lecture Notes in Computer Science (LNCS) style](https://www.springer.com/gp/computer-science/lncs/conference-proceedings-guidelines).
- **Review Process:** Submissions will be subject to a double-blind review process by three reviewers selected to avoid institutional conflict.
- **Ethics & Privacy:** Papers involving patient data must clearly state ethical approval or waiver status. Any use of synthetic or simulated data must be explicitly identified.
- **Submission System:** Microsoft CMT (Portal link TBD).
- **Proceedings:** Accepted papers will be published following the MICCAI Springer’s joint publication model.
